About this tool

Estimate the total of the main severance payments due in case of unfair dismissal: salary balance, notice period, accrued and proportional vacation + 1/3, proportional 13th salary and 40% fine on FGTS. Useful for nursing professionals and other workers to check an estimate of the total amount to receive in case of unfair dismissal.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–99999999: Estimated Total Severance Amount

Important tips

  • This calculator only covers dismissal without just cause — resignation and dismissal for just cause have very different entitlements (e.g., no paid notice period from the employer and no FGTS fine).
  • The proportional notice period is limited to 90 total days by law, even if the simple calculation (30 + 3×years) exceeds this value for workers with more than 20 years at the company.
  • Remember to include averages of habitual overtime and night shift premiums in the actual calculation base, which are not covered in this simplified estimate.
  • The Termination of Employment Contract (TRCT) must detail each payment separately — use it for verification.

    Foundation

    The calculation of severance pay for unfair dismissal sums various items: salary balance (days worked in the month), indemnified notice period (30 days plus 3 days per full year of service, according to Law No. 12,506/2011, limited to 90 days), accrued and proportional vacation plus 1/3 constitutional addition, proportional 13th salary, and termination fine of 40% on the FGTS balance (Art. 18, §1, Law No. 8,036/1990).

    History

    Each type of dismissal (unfair dismissal, resignation, dismissal for cause, mutual agreement between employee and employer according to Art. 484-A of CLT) entitles different benefits. This calculator only covers the scenario of unfair dismissal by employer initiative, the most common and complete in terms of rights.

    Scientific Validation

    The actual amount may vary according to collective bargaining agreements, averages of habitual additional payments (overtime, night shift premium, commissions) that must compose the calculation basis, and possible deductions (social security, income tax, advances, transportation vouchers).

    Limitations

    This calculator provides a simplified estimate for educational purposes, considering only the scenario of dismissal without just cause. It does not include INSS/IRRF discounts, averages of habitual additional payments, nor does it cover other types of termination (resignation, dismissal for just cause, mutual agreement, end of probationary contract), which have their own rules. It does not replace the official termination calculation or guidance from an accounting or labor law professional.

    How the Termination Calculation Works

    This calculator sums the main payments of a dismissal without just cause: outstanding salary, paid notice period (proportional to years of service), accrued and proportional vacation plus 1/3, proportional 13th salary, and 40% fine on the FGTS balance.

    Attention: this is a simplified educational calculation. It does not replace the official Termination of Employment Contract (TRCT) or guidance from an accounting or labor law professional.

    Does this calculator work for resignation or dismissal for just cause?

    No. This calculator only covers the scenario of dismissal without just cause initiated by the employer. Resignation and dismissal for just cause follow different rules, with reduced entitlements (no paid notice period from the employer and no FGTS fine).

    Is the result of this calculator the exact amount I will receive?

    No. It is a simplified educational estimate. The actual amount may vary depending on INSS/IRRF discounts, averages of habitual additional payments, and other contractual specifics. Consult the official Termination of Employment Contract (TRCT) or an accounting/labor law professional for the exact amount.
