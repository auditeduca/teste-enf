About this tool

To screen the risk of obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) based on 8 simple clinical criteria, guiding the need for further investigation (polysomnography) and perioperative care. Indicated in pre-anesthetic assessment, primary care screening, and whenever there is clinical suspicion of obstructive sleep apnea.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–2: Low Risk
  • 3–4: Intermediate Risk
  • 5–8: High Risk

Important tips

  • STOP-BANG is especially useful in pre-anesthetic assessment to anticipate special airway and sedation care.
  • Scores of 5 or more have good sensitivity for moderate to severe obstructive sleep apnea.
  • Always refer for polysomnography when there is high clinical suspicion, regardless of the score.
  • Advise on modifiable risk factors such as weight loss and reduction of alcohol consumption before bedtime.

    Foundation

    The STOP-BANG questionnaire was developed by Frances Chung and colleagues as a simple screening tool for obstructive sleep apnea, combining 4 symptom questions (Snoring, Tiredness, Observed apnea, Pressure) with 4 demographic and anthropometric characteristics (BMI, Age, Neck circumference, Gender).

    History

    Widely used in pre-anesthetic assessment to identify patients at risk of undiagnosed obstructive sleep apnea, guiding specific perioperative care to prevent respiratory complications.

    NANDA — Ineffective breathing pattern

    Inspiration and/or expiration that does not provide adequate ventilation, associated with upper airway obstruction during sleep.

    NANDA — Disturbed Sleep Pattern

    Time-limited interruptions in the quantity and quality of sleep due to intrinsic factors.

    NANDA — Fatigue

    Overwhelming and sustained sense of exhaustion and decreased capacity for physical and mental work, associated with poor sleep quality.

    NOC — Sleep

    • Sleep quality reported as adequate
    • Absence of excessive daytime sleepiness
    • Absence of observed apnea episodes

    NIC — Sleep Enhancement

    • Administer the STOP-BANG questionnaire for risk screening
    • Refer for specialist evaluation and polysomnography when indicated
    • Educate about modifiable risk factors (weight, sleep position, alcohol before sleep)
    • Reinforce perioperative care in high-risk patients (airway monitoring and sedation)

    Scientific Validation

    Extensively validated in the literature, with good sensitivity to detect moderate to severe obstructive sleep apnea, especially at higher scores (5 or more).

    Limitations

    It is a screening tool, not replacing definitive diagnosis by polysomnography. It may have a relatively high rate of false-positive results, especially at intermediate scores.

    How STOP-BANG works

    STOP-BANG adds 1 point for each of the 8 criteria present: loud snoring, daytime tiredness, observed apnea, hypertension, BMI > 35, age > 50 years, neck circumference > 40 cm, and male sex, totaling 0 to 8 points.

    Quick guide: 0-2 = Low risk | 3-4 = Intermediate risk | 5-8 = High risk of obstructive sleep apnea.

    Does STOP-BANG diagnose obstructive sleep apnea?

    No. It is a screening tool. Definitive diagnosis requires polysomnography, especially in patients classified as intermediate or high risk.

    Why is STOP-BANG important before surgery?

    Patients with undiagnosed obstructive sleep apnea have a higher risk of perioperative respiratory complications, especially related to sedation and airway management. Identifying this risk beforehand allows adjustment of the anesthetic plan and postoperative monitoring.
