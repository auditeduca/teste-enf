Jouvet Scale

Assessment of Level of Consciousness

The Jouvet Coma Scale is an instrument used to assess a patient's level of consciousness, especially in cases of coma induced by neurological diseases or persistent vegetative states. It focuses on evaluating the patient's perceptiveness and reactivity, both to cortical and brainstem stimuli. Unlike the Glasgow Coma Scale, the Jouvet scale is more commonly used to assess patients in deeper states of altered consciousness, such as coma.

Year of Creation: Between the 1960s and 1970s.

Authors: Professor Michel Jouvet

Area of Use: Intensive care unit (ICU).

Criteria assessed: Perceptiveness, Nonspecific Reactivity, Specific Reactivity to Pain, and Autonomic Reactivity.

Who uses it?: Intensivists, neurologists, and intensive care nurses.
