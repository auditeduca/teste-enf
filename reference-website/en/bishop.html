About this tool

Assess cervical favorability for labor induction, based on 5 parameters of cervical examination and station of the fetal presenting part. Indicated before elective or medically indicated labor induction to estimate the probability of successful induction.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–5: Unfavorable Cervix
  • 6–8: Intermediate Cervix
  • 9–13: Favorable Cervix

Important tips

  • Lower scores (unfavorable cervix) generally indicate the need for cervical ripening before induction with oxytocin.
  • Reassess the Bishop Score if there is a significant change in cervical examination over follow-up.
  • Always combine with continuous fetal monitoring during the induction process.
  • The score should be interpreted within the complete clinical context of the pregnant woman, not in isolation.

    Foundation

    The Bishop Score was developed by Edward Bishop in 1964, evaluating 5 parameters of cervical examination (dilation, effacement, consistency, and position) and fetal station to estimate cervical favorability for labor induction.

    History

    Widely used in obstetrics as a standard criterion for decision-making regarding the method of labor induction (direct induction with oxytocin versus need for prior cervical ripening with prostaglandins or mechanical methods).

    NANDA — Readiness for enhanced childbearing process

    A pattern of preparing for and protecting pregnancy, gestation, and the parental role that can be strengthened.

    NANDA — Anxiety

    Vague, uneasy feeling of discomfort or dread related to the expectation of labor and induction.

    NOC — Preparation for childbirth

    • Understands the labor induction process
    • Stable maternal and fetal vital signs
    • Participates in decisions about the birth plan

    NIC — Induction of labor

    • Assess cervical favorability with Bishop Score before induction
    • Monitor fetal heart rate and uterine activity during induction
    • Educate the pregnant woman about the induction process and warning signs
    • Communicate the evolution of the condition to the obstetric team

    Scientific Validation

    Extensively validated in obstetric literature, with good correlation between low scores and higher risk of induction failure (cesarean delivery), being one of the most used instruments worldwide in this assessment.

    Limitations

    It is an instrument with a certain degree of subjectivity in cervical examination, potentially presenting interobserver variability. It should not be the sole criterion for decision-making regarding induction, but rather combined with a complete clinical assessment of the pregnant woman and fetus.

    How the Bishop Score Works

    The Bishop Score sums points from 5 parameters — dilation, effacement, consistency, and position of the cervix, as well as the station of the fetal presenting part — totaling 0 to 13 points, which indicate cervical favorability for labor induction.

    Quick guide: 0-5 = Unfavorable | 6-8 = Intermediate | 9-13 = Favorable.

    Does an unfavorable cervix (low Bishop) prevent labor induction?

    It does not necessarily prevent it, but it generally indicates the need for cervical ripening methods before direct induction with oxytocin, as assessed by the obstetric team.

    Is the Bishop Score the only criterion for deciding on labor induction?

    No. It should be combined with a complete clinical assessment of the pregnant woman and fetus, including the indication for induction, gestational age, and maternal and fetal conditions.
