About this tool

To assess balance and gait in older adults through standardized functional maneuvers, estimating fall risk. Indicated in geriatric assessment of older adults, especially those with history of falls, gait or balance disorders, or as part of a comprehensive functional assessment.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–18: High Fall Risk
  • 19–23: Moderate Fall Risk
  • 24–28: Low Fall Risk

Important tips

  • The scale is divided into two parts: balance (maximum 16 points) and gait (maximum 12 points), totaling 28 points.
  • Use a rigid chair without armrests and a path of approximately 3 meters for standardized assessment.
  • The cutoff of 18 points or less for high risk is widely used in clinical practice.
  • Combine Tinetti results with other tools (Timed Up and Go, fall history) for a more comprehensive risk assessment.

    Foundation

    The Tinetti Scale (Performance-Oriented Mobility Assessment - POMA) evaluates 9 balance maneuvers (maximum 16 points) and 8 gait maneuvers (maximum 12 points), totaling a combined score from 0 to 28 points, to assess fall risk in older adults.

    History

    Developed by Mary Tinetti in 1986, it is one of the most widely used tools worldwide for functional assessment of balance and gait in older adults, extensively employed in geriatrics and physical therapy.

    NANDA — Risk for falls

    Increased susceptibility to falls, which may cause physical harm and compromise health, related to impaired balance and/or gait.

    NANDA — Impaired physical mobility

    Limitation in independent, intentional physical movement of the body or one or more extremities.

    NANDA — Impaired Walking

    Limitation of independent ambulation through the environment.

    NOC — Balance

    • Tinetti score assessed and monitored over time
    • Absence of falls during hospitalization/follow-up period
    • Participation in balance exercise program when indicated

    NIC — Fall prevention

    • Apply the Tinetti Scale in balance and gait assessment
    • Refer to physical therapy according to identified risk
    • Educate on proper use of gait assistive devices
    • Adapt the environment to reduce fall risks (lighting, rugs, footwear)
    • Review medications associated with fall risk (sedatives, antihypertensives)

    Scientific Validation

    Widely validated and used in clinical practice and research, with good interrater reliability and correlation with other mobility measures such as the Timed Up and Go and gait speed.

    Limitations

    Requires training and time for administration (10-15 minutes). The gait subtest may have a ceiling effect in populations with relatively preserved function. Cutoff points may vary across studies and populations.

    How the Tinetti Scale (POMA) Works

    The Tinetti Scale assesses balance (9 maneuvers, maximum 16 points) and gait (8 items, maximum 12 points), summing to a total score from 0 to 28 points.

    Quick guide: ≤18 = High fall risk | 19-23 = Moderate risk | ≥24 = Low risk.

    How long does it take to administer the Tinetti Scale?

    The complete application usually takes 10 to 15 minutes, including observation of balance maneuvers (sitting, standing up, standing, turning) and gait (walking about 3 meters).

    Does the Tinetti Scale replace other fall risk assessments?

    Not alone. It is recommended to combine its results with history of previous falls, medication assessment, muscle strength, and other tools such as the Timed Up and Go for a more complete fall risk assessment.
