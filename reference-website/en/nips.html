About this tool

Assess pain intensity in newborns through behavioral indicators (facial expression, cry, arm and leg movement, state of consciousness) and physiological indicators (breathing pattern). Indicated for pain assessment in term and preterm newborns, especially during painful procedures and in the postoperative period.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–2: No Pain or Minimal Pain
  • 3–4: Moderate Pain
  • 5–7: Severe Pain

Important tips

  • The NIPS combines behavioral indicators (facial expression, cry, arms, legs, state of consciousness) and one physiological indicator (breathing pattern).
  • Cry is the only item scored from 0 to 2; the others are scored from 0 to 1.
  • Assess pain before, during, and after painful procedures to monitor the effectiveness of interventions.
  • Always combine non-pharmacological measures (non-nutritive sucking, oral glucose, holding) with pharmacological analgesia when indicated.

    Foundation

    NIPS assesses 6 indicators — facial expression, cry, breathing pattern, arms, legs, and state of consciousness — most scored 0 to 1, except cry (0 to 2), totaling a score of 0 to 7 points.

    History

    Desenvolvida no Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario, é uma das escalas comportamentais de dor mais utilizadas em unidades neonatais, por sua simplicidade e rapidez de aplicação.

    NANDA — Acute pain

    Unpleasant sensory and emotional experience associated with actual or potential tissue damage, of sudden onset, identified by behavioral and physiological indicators in the newborn.

    NOC — Pain control

    • NIPS score assessed systematically before, during, and after painful procedures
    • Uses pharmacologic and non-pharmacologic relief measures as indicated
    • Reduction in NIPS score after intervention

    NIC — Neonatal Pain Management

    • Apply NIPS before, during, and after potentially painful procedures
    • Use non-pharmacological comfort measures (non-nutritive sucking, oral glucose, facilitated containment, holding/kangaroo care)
    • Administer analgesia as prescribed for high scores
    • Involve parents in pain management and newborn comfort
    • Record NIPS score in the medical record

    Scientific Validation

    Validated and translated into several languages, including Brazilian Portuguese, with good applicability in term and preterm newborns, both for acute procedural pain and in the postoperative period.

    Limitations

    It may be influenced by factors such as hunger, sleep, and extreme prematurity, which can alter the behavioral expressiveness of pain. In newborns under neuromuscular blockade or deep sedation, interpretation should be cautious.

    How the Neonatal Pain Scale (NIPS) Works

    The NIPS sums the score of 6 indicators — facial expression, cry, breathing pattern, arms, legs, and state of consciousness — totaling 0 to 7 points (cry scores 0 to 2, the others 0 to 1).

    Quick guide: 0-2 = No pain/minimal | 3-4 = Moderate pain | 5-7 = Severe pain.

    Can the NIPS be used in older infants, beyond the neonatal period?

    The scale was developed and validated primarily for newborns, generally applicable up to about 1 year of age. For infants and older children, other scales such as FLACC are usually more appropriate.

    Can factors like hunger or sleep influence the NIPS score?

    Yes. As with other behavioral scales, hunger, sleep, and extreme prematurity can alter the behavioral expressiveness of the newborn, so it is important to consider the complete clinical context when interpreting the score.
