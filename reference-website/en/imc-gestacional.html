About this tool

Calculate the pregnant woman's Body Mass Index (BMI) and provide guidance on nutritional status classification, aiding in prenatal follow-up and defining gestational weight gain goals. Indicated in prenatal care for assessment and monitoring of the pregnant woman's nutritional status throughout pregnancy.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–18.4: Underweight
  • 18.5–24.9: Adequate Weight
  • 25–29.9: Overweight
  • 30–70: Obesity

Important tips

  • Gestational BMI uses the same formula as conventional BMI: weight (kg) divided by height (m) squared.
  • Ideally, BMI should be calculated using pre-pregnancy weight or weight obtained at the first prenatal visit, to correctly define the weight gain goal.
  • For more accurate monitoring throughout pregnancy, use specific BMI-for-gestational-age curves (Atalah curve), adopted by the Ministry of Health.
  • Pregnant women with low weight or obesity should be referred for specialized nutritional follow-up.

    Foundation

    Gestational BMI is calculated the same way as conventional BMI (weight in kg divided by height in meters squared), being used at the beginning of pregnancy to classify pre-gestational nutritional status and guide weight gain goals throughout pregnancy.

    History

    Recommendations for gestational weight gain by pre-pregnancy BMI category were established by institutions such as the Institute of Medicine (IOM), and are adopted and adapted by prenatal guidelines, including those of the Brazilian Ministry of Health.

    NANDA — Imbalanced Nutrition: Less Than Body Requirements

    Insufficient nutrient intake to meet metabolic needs, related to gestational underweight.

    NANDA — Risk for imbalanced nutrition: more than body requirements

    Risk of nutrient intake exceeding metabolic needs, related to gestational overweight/obesity.

    NANDA — Readiness for enhanced nutrition

    Pattern of nutrient intake sufficient to meet metabolic needs, which can be strengthened, applicable to pregnant women with adequate weight seeking healthy maintenance.

    NOC — Nutritional status

    • Gestational BMI monitored throughout prenatal care
    • Gestational weight gain within the recommended goal for nutritional classification
    • Absence of complications related to nutritional status

    NIC — Nutritional counseling

    • Calculate and monitor gestational BMI at each prenatal visit
    • Provide guidance on gestational weight gain goal according to nutritional classification
    • Refer for specialized nutritional follow-up when indicated
    • Monitor signs of complications associated with nutritional status (gestational diabetes, hypertension)
    • Provide guidance on balanced diet and physical activity compatible with pregnancy

    Scientific Validation

    BMI classification and associated weight gain goals are widely adopted in prenatal protocols, although more accurate monitoring throughout pregnancy uses specific curves (such as the Atalah curve), which consider BMI as a function of gestational age, not just the isolated value.

    Limitations

    This calculator applies the standard BMI formula (weight/height²), useful mainly as an initial assessment. For monitoring the adequacy of weight gain throughout pregnancy, the use of specific BMI-for-gestational-age curves (Atalah curve), adopted by the Ministry of Health, is recommended, which are not incorporated in this simplified calculator.

    How Gestational BMI Calculation Works

    Gestational BMI is calculated by dividing weight (kg) by height squared (m²), the same as conventional BMI, and is used to classify the pregnant woman's nutritional status and guide weight gain goals during pregnancy.

    Quick guide: <18.5 = Underweight | 18.5-24.9 = Adequate | 25-29.9 = Overweight | ≥30 = Obesity.

    Should I use current weight or pre-pregnancy weight to calculate gestational BMI?

    Ideally, pre-pregnancy weight (or weight obtained at the first prenatal visit) is used to classify initial nutritional status and define the appropriate weight gain goal for the remainder of pregnancy.

    Does this calculator consider gestational age in BMI classification?

    No. This calculator applies the standard BMI formula, useful primarily as an initial assessment. For monitoring weight adequacy throughout pregnancy, it is ideal to use specific BMI-for-gestational-age curves, such as the Atalah curve adopted by the Ministry of Health.
