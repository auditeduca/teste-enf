5 Hs of Cardiac Arrest: Causes and Treatment

Cardiac Arrest: The 5 Most Likely Causes (5 H’s)

Clinical approach to the main probable causes of cardiac arrest starting with the letter "H" according to AHA guidelines

Os The 5 H's of cardiac arrest are five words starting with the letter "H" along with the 5 T's of cardiac arrest, they represent the main probable causes of cardiac arrest that must be quickly identified and corrected during emergency care. This concept is fundamental in the ACLS (Advanced Cardiac Life Support) protocol and guides the systematic approach during cardiopulmonary resuscitation. Each word starting with the letter "H" corresponds to a specific condition that, if corrected, can result in reversal of arrest and restoration of spontaneous circulation. In this article, we will discuss the 5 H's.

The 5 Hs of Cardiac Arrest

H₁ - HYPOXIA
H₂ - HYPOVOLEMIA
H₃ - HYPOTHERMIA
H₄ - POTASSIUM
H₅ - ACIDOSIS (H⁺)

What are the 5 Hs?

The 5 Hs are five words starting with the letter "H" for metabolic and physiological conditions that can cause or contribute to cardiac arrest. They are memorized by the acronym "H's": Hypothermia, Hypokalemia/Hyperkalemia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hypoxia, Hypovolemia. Rapid identification and correction of these factors is essential to increase survival chances and reduce neurological sequelae.

1st H - HYPOXIA (Lack of Oxygen)

A Hypoxia is the first cause to be investigated in cardiac arrest. The brain is extremely sensitive to lack of oxygen, potentially suffering irreversible damage within 4-6 minutes. Hypoxia can result from:

  • Upper airway obstruction
  • Respiratory depression due to drugs (opioids, sedatives)
  • Pulmonary edema or pneumothorax
  • Carbon monoxide poisoning
  • Drowning or near-drowning

Treatment of Hypoxia:

  • Ensure patent airway: Chin lift, check for obstruction, perform orotracheal suctioning if necessary
  • Adequate ventilation: If in cardiac arrest, before advanced airway, a professional is needed to bag-mask ventilate, 2 breaths every 30 chest compressions.
  • Oxygen therapy: Initially O2 catheter, may progress to mechanical ventilation
  • In the operating room: Orotracheal intubation is likely already established; if not, nurse support alongside the anesthesiologist is important.
  • Reversal of sedation: Naloxone (Narcan) for opioid overdose, this is per medical order
  • Suctioning: Keep vacuum system ready. If intubation occurs, suction the upper airway before the procedure.

Important Note:

Finger pulse oximetry may provide false readings in cases of hypoperfusion. More appropriate methods include: assessment of chest expansion, capnography, and arterial blood gas analysis to evaluate effective oxygen delivery.

2nd H - HYPOVOLEMIA (Low Blood Volume)

A Hypovolemia is the second cause to be checked. It can be caused by external or internal hemorrhages, severe dehydration, or extensive burns. Insufficient blood volume compromises tissue perfusion and oxygen delivery to vital organs.

Common causes:

  • Traumatic hemorrhages
  • Ruptured aneurysms
  • Gastrointestinal bleeding
  • Severe dehydration
  • Extensive burns

Hypovolemia Treatment:

  1. Blood Product Transfusion: Perform blood typing collection; in case of emergency, a liability waiver justifying infusion of O- packed red blood cells
  2. Crystalloids: Lactated Ringer's for volume resuscitation, replaces lost volume and electrolytes, and can correct metabolic acidosis
  3. Hemorrhage Control: Direct compression, tourniquet, emergency operating room to control hemorrhage
  4. Cautious Infusion of Normal Saline: Up to 2000 mL (rapid infusion), prioritizing blood products thereafter per AHA guidelines
  5. Permissive Hypotension: Intensive patient assessment by the attending physician, considering acceptable hypotension values under continuous monitoring.
  6. Watch for Signs: Pay close attention to blood pressure in hypovolemia; it leads to hypotension and, compensatorily, initially tachycardia followed by bradycardia, weak and thready pulse, tachypnea, decreased level of consciousness, hypoglycemia, and hypothermia

3rd H - HYPOTHERMIA (Low Body Temperature)

A Hypothermia (temperatura corporal < 35°C) causa alterações significativas no sistema cardiovascular e neurológico, reduz o ritmo cardíaco, diminui a perfusão tecidual e stá associada a distúrbios elétricos que podem evoluir de taquicardia inicial para bradicardia e, posteriormente, para ritmo chocável.

Effects of Hypothermia:

  • Decreased cerebral metabolism due to reduced perfusion
  • Alterations in cardiac electrical conduction
  • Ineffective reduction of gas exchange
  • Central nervous system depression

Hypothermia Treatment:

  • Electric heating blanket: Most efficient warming method
  • Warmed normal saline: Fluids warmed to 40-42°C
  • Active warming: Chemical blankets, portable heaters
  • Cardiac monitoring: Continuous ECG due to risk of arrhythmias
  • Environment: Turn off air conditioning if possible

4th H - POTASSIUM DISORDERS

Os Potassium disturbances são críticos na PCR. Tanto a hipocalemia (< 2,7 mEq/L) quanto a hipercalemia (> 6,0 mEq/L) podem causar arritmias fatais e comprometer a contratilidade cardíaca. A hipocalemia frequentemente está associada à hipomagnesemia.

4.1 Hipocalemia (< 2,7 mEq/L):

Treatment:

  • Magnesium Replacement: The person responsible for the emergency cart, considering the 5 Hs as causes of cardiac arrest, should be wise and keep magnesium ampoules, tubing, and infusion pump easily accessible
  • K+ Replacement: Not recommended during cardiac arrest per AHA 2010/2021
  • Efficiency: Nursing professional: having the patient's recent lab results at hand and having collected blood for laboratory testing at the onset of clinical deterioration can ensure a good outcome for the patient.

4.2 Hipercalemia (> 6,0 mEq/L):

Calcium gluconate is a K+ antagonist; in case of hyperkalemia, your emergency cart should have this medication readily visible:

  1. Calcium Gluconate: 10-20 mL IV over 2-5 minutes
  2. Insulin + Glucose: Facilitates K+ entry into cells
  3. Sodium Bicarbonate: Raises blood pH, promotes K+ entry into cells
  4. Diuretics or resin: For removal of K+ from the body
  5. Dialysis: In severe and refractory cases
  6. Emergency cart responsible: always keep these medications on top of your emergency cart, organize your care

5th H - METABOLIC ACIDOSIS (H+)

A Metabolic acidosis (pH < 7,35) resulta do acúmulo de ácidos ou perda de bicarbonato. É comumente associada ao diabetes mellitus descompensado e doença renal crônica. O ECG pode mostrar baixa amplitude das ondas.

Causes of metabolic acidosis:

  • Diabetic ketoacidosis
  • Renal failure
  • Lactic acidosis (tissue hypoxia)
  • Toxin ingestion (methanol, ethylene glycol)
  • Severe diarrhea

Acidosis Treatment:

  • Sodium Bicarbonate: Indicado para pH < 6,7 ou > 7,2
  • Correction of underlying cause: Treat the cause of acidosis
  • Adequate ventilation: Respiratory compensation
  • Monitoring: Serial arterial blood gas

Protocol for Approaching the 5 Hs

Order of Investigation and Treatment:

1 HYPOXIA - Ensure adequate oxygenation
2 HYPOVOLEMIA - Replace blood volume
3 HYPOTHERMIA - Rewarm the patient
4 POTASSIUM - Correct electrolyte disturbances
5 ACIDOSIS - Correct blood pH

Clinical Importance of the 5 Hs

Memorizing and systematically applying the 5 Hs during CPR is essential to increase survival rates and reduce neurological sequelae. Each condition represents a reversible factor that, if identified and corrected quickly, can result in the restoration of spontaneous circulation. In-depth knowledge of these concepts is essential for all healthcare professionals involved in emergency care.
