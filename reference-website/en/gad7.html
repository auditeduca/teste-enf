About this tool

Screen and quantify the severity of anxiety symptoms over the past two weeks, based on 7 items related to Generalized Anxiety Disorder. Indicated for screening anxiety symptoms in primary care, nursing consultations, and monitoring mental health treatment.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–4: Minimal Anxiety
  • 5–9: Mild Anxiety
  • 10–14: Moderate Anxiety
  • 15–21: Severe Anxiety

Important tips

  • The GAD-7 is also useful for screening other anxiety disorders besides GAD, such as panic disorder and social anxiety.
  • Apply in a private and welcoming environment, explaining the purpose of the questionnaire before starting.
  • Reassess periodically to monitor treatment response over time.
  • The instrument does not replace complete clinical and diagnostic evaluation by a qualified mental health professional.

    Foundation

    The GAD-7 was developed by Spitzer, Kroenke, Williams, and Löwe as a brief screening instrument for Generalized Anxiety Disorder, assessing 7 core anxiety symptoms over the past 2 weeks.

    History

    Widely used worldwide in primary care and mental health services, also useful for screening other anxiety disorders beyond GAD, such as panic disorder and social anxiety, and is in the public domain for clinical, educational, and research use.

    NANDA — Anxiety

    Vague and uncomfortable feeling of discomfort or fear, accompanied by autonomic response; feeling of apprehension caused by anticipation of danger.

    NANDA — Disturbed sleep pattern

    Time-limited interruptions in sleep quantity and quality due to external factors.

    NANDA — Ineffective Coping

    Inability to form a valid appraisal of stressors, inadequate choices of practical responses, and/or inability to use available resources.

    NOC — Anxiety level

    • Verbalizes feelings appropriately
    • Uses effective coping strategies
    • Maintains adequate sleep pattern
    • Participates in usual activities

    NIC — Anxiety reduction

    • Apply the GAD-7 systematically in patients with suspected anxiety
    • Refer for mental health evaluation according to identified severity
    • Provide guidance on stress management and relaxation techniques
    • Periodically reassess during follow-up

    Scientific Validation

    Extensively validated in diverse populations and languages, including Brazilian Portuguese, with good sensitivity and specificity for identifying Generalized Anxiety Disorder.

    Limitations

    It is a screening instrument, not a substitute for formal clinical diagnosis, which must be performed by a qualified professional. Anxiety symptoms may overlap with other clinical and psychiatric conditions, requiring careful differential evaluation.

    How the GAD-7 works

    The GAD-7 sums points from 7 items related to core anxiety symptoms, each scored from 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day), referring to the past 2 weeks, totaling 0 to 21 points.

    Quick guide: 0-4 = Minimal | 5-9 = Mild | 10-14 = Moderate | 15-21 = Severe.

    Is the GAD-7 a diagnostic instrument for anxiety?

    No. It is a screening and severity monitoring instrument for anxiety symptoms. Formal diagnosis must be performed by a qualified mental health professional based on a complete clinical evaluation.

    Can the GAD-7 be used together with the PHQ-9?

    Yes. It is common to apply both instruments together in mental health screening, as anxiety and depression often coexist and overlap clinically.
