Surgical Instruments: Complete List for Operating Room

ESSENTIAL SURGICAL INSTRUMENTS FOR PERFORMING SURGERIES IN THE OPERATING ROOM

The instruments are listed in the sequence of surgical time: Diérese , Hemostasis , Exeresis e Synthesis . Also included is the list of important auxiliary materials for the pre and intraoperative period.

DIÉRESE
(Instruments for Incision, Cutting and Divulsion of Tissues)

SCALPEL AND ACCESSORIES

• SCALPEL (Scalpel Handle)
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Basic instrument for performing surgical incisions. Used in all specialties to cut skin, subcutaneous tissue, and soft tissues. It comes in different numbers (3, 4, 7) depending on the blade to be used and the type of procedure.

• SCALPEL BLADE (No. 10, 11, 12, 15, 20, 21, 22, 24)
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Disposable sharp blades that fit onto the scalpel handle. Each number has a specific shape:

  • No. 10: curvilinear shape for large incisions
  • No. 11: pointed shape for punctures and precise incisions
  • No. 15: small curvilinear shape for small incisions
  • No. 20-24: shape for more extensive procedures
• ELECTROSURGICAL PENCIL (High-Frequency Scalpel)
Phase: DIÉRESE / HEMOSTASIS

Function: Equipment that uses high-frequency electrical current to cut tissues and simultaneously promote coagulation of blood vessels. Used in virtually all surgical specialties as an alternative or complement to the conventional scalpel.

SURGICAL SCISSORS

• MAYO SCISSORS - STRAIGHT
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Robust scissors with straight blades and blunt tips. Used for cutting denser tissues such as fascia, muscles, and sutures.

• MAYO SCISSORS - CURVED
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Scissors with curved blades that allow access to areas of difficult visualization. Ideal for cutting tissues in cavities.

• METZENBAUM SCISSORS - STRAIGHT
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Delicate scissors with long, thin blades. Indicated for dissection and incision of delicate tissues.

• METZENBAUM SCISSORS - CURVED
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Same function as straight Metzenbaum, but with curved blades that facilitate access to deep anatomical spaces.

• IRIS SCISSORS - STRAIGHT
Phase: DIÉRESE / SYNTHESIS

Function: Small, delicate scissors with fine tips. Used in ophthalmology and microsurgery.

• IRIS SCISSORS - CURVED
Phase: DIÉRESE / SYNTHESIS

Function: Curved version of Iris scissors, allowing greater precision in angled procedures.

• STEVENS SCISSORS
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Dissection scissors with straight or curved blades and fine tips for precise dissection.

• WESTCOTT SCISSORS
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Specific scissors for ophthalmology, allowing precise incisions in the cornea and sclera.

• CASTROVIEJO SCISSORS
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Delicate scissors with spring mechanism for microsurgery.

• JAMESON SCISSORS
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Ophthalmic scissors for cutting ocular muscles.

• POTTS SCISSORS (Potts-Smith)
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Angled scissors for vascular procedures (arteriotomies).

• SUTURE SCISSORS (Spencer)
Phase: SYNTHESIS

Function: Specific scissors for removing suture stitches.

• GIANT SCISSORS
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Large scissors for cutting dense tissues in extensive procedures.

SPECIAL CUTTING INSTRUMENTS

• GIGLI SAW
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Steel wire saw used for cutting bones.

• GOUGE (Osteotome)
Phase: DIÉRESE (EXERESIS)

Function: Cutting instrument for removing bone fragments.

• CORNEAL TREPHINE
Phase: DIÉRESE / EXERESIS

Function: Cylindrical cutting instrument for removing corneal discs.

• ELECTROCAUTERY (Electrosurgical Unit)
Phase: DIÉRESE / HEMOSTASIS

Function: Instrument that uses electrical current for cutting and coagulation.

SURGICAL BURS AND DRILLS

• SURGICAL BUR
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Rotary instrument for drilling bones.

• SURGICAL BURR
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Burs for modeling and finishing bone surfaces.

• HUDSON DRILL
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Orthopedic drill for perforating long bones.

• STEINMANN PIN
Phase: DIÉRESE

Function: Thick drill for bone perforation in external fixation procedures.

HEMOSTASIS
(Instruments for Bleeding Control and Prevention)

HEMOSTATIC CLAMPS

• KELLY CLAMP STRAIGHT
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Hemostatic clamp with partial serration. Used for clamping medium-caliber vessels.

• KELLY CLAMP CURVED
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Same function as straight Kelly, but curved for deep access.

• CRILE CLAMP STRAIGHT
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Robust hemostatic clamp with full serration. Indicated for larger vessels.

• CRILE CLAMP CURVED
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Curved version of Crile, greater versatility.

• MOSQUITO CLAMP STRAIGHT
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Delicate hemostatic clamp for small vessels.

• MOSQUITO CLAMP CURVED
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Curved version of Mosquito for precise access.

• HALSTEAD CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Similar to Mosquito, specific design for greater firmness.

• PEAN CLAMP STRAIGHT
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Robust clamp with full serration for large vessels.

• PEAN CLAMP CURVED
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Curved version of Pean.

• ROCHESTER PEAN CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Large hemostatic clamp for extensive procedures.

• ROCHESTER OCHSNER CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Robust clamp with teeth at the tip for dense tissues.

• WERTHEIM CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Long, robust clamp for deep pelvic procedures.

• HEANEY CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Curved hemostatic clamp specific for gynecology (hysterectomy).

• SCHROEDER CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Hemostatic clamp for obstetric and gynecologic procedures.

• SATINSKY CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Partial hemostatic clamp for vascular sutures.

• BULLDOG CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Delicate clamp for temporary vessel occlusion.

SPECIALIZED HEMOSTATIC CLAMP

• BABCOCK CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS / GRASPING

Function: Clamp with rounded, serrated tips for delicate grasping (intestine).

• ALLIS CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS / GRASPING

• KOCHER CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

Function: Hemostatic clamp with teeth at the tip for greater firmness.

• MIXTER CLAMP
Phase: HEMOSTASIS

• DEBAKEY CLAMP

• POTTS-SMITH CLAMP

ESSENTIAL CRITICAL AND SEMI-CRITICAL ITEMS DURING SURGERY
(Cannulas, Tubes, Basins, Bowls)

• LATEX SUCTION TUBE
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: May be used during intubation by the anesthesiologist and during surgeries for fluid drainage.

• KIDNEY BASIN
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Used to collect fluids, body waste (such as urine), medications, saline, or alcohol during pre- and intraoperative procedures.

• SURGICAL BASIN
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Used in major surgeries to place organs, tissues, foreign bodies, or large anatomical specimens.

• ROUND BASIN
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Used to hold aseptic liquids such as chlorhexidine, iodine, or saline solutions.

• LARYNGOSCOPE BLADES
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: The anesthesiologist will use the correct size to visualize the patient's tracheal opening and perform orotracheal intubation before surgery.

• GUEDEL AIRWAY
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Used to open the airway and improve oxygen flow to the upper airway.

• INTUBATION GUIDE WIRE
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: It is inserted inside the endotracheal tube to assist the anesthesiologist during intubation.

• INTUBATION BOUGIE
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Inserted before the endotracheal tube upon visualization of the trachea during intubation; after its placement, the endotracheal tube is introduced over the tip of the Bougie and guided into proper position. After tube placement, the Bougie is removed. Being extremely flexible, it is a very important tool among intubation materials.

• SURGICAL TRAY
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Rectangular in shape, it assists the surgeon in keeping instruments that have been used and will still be used in a sterile and easily accessible container.

• SUCTION CATHETER
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Drainage of secretions, used in the pre-, intra-, and postoperative periods for drainage of airway secretions.

• METAL TRACHEOSTOMY CANNULA
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Long-term sterile instrument inserted by the surgeon via tracheostomy.

• COLONOSCOPES, ENDOSCOPES, AND BRONCHOSCOPES
Phase: ASSISTANT

Function: Semiflexible tubes with a camera tip for visualization of regions such as the stomach, intestine, and bronchi; they have an outlet for liquid aspiration, air insufflation, and an inlet for probes to perform invasive procedures.
