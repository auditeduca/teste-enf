About this tool

Perform rapid bedside screening to identify patients with suspected infection at risk of unfavorable outcome (sepsis), outside the intensive care setting. Indicated in emergency departments, wards, and any non-ICU environment, in adult patients with suspected infection.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–1: Low Risk
  • 2–3: High Risk

Important tips

  • qSOFA does not replace complete clinical assessment nor institutional sepsis protocols.
  • A low qSOFA does not rule out sepsis — maintain vigilance if strong clinical suspicion of infection exists.
  • Use in conjunction with serum lactate and SIRS criteria when available.
  • Reassess the score with each change in the patient's clinical condition.

    Foundation

    qSOFA (quick Sequential Organ Failure Assessment) was proposed in the Third International Consensus Definitions for Sepsis and Septic Shock (Sepsis-3, 2016) as a simple bedside screening tool outside the ICU to identify patients with suspected infection at risk of unfavorable outcome.

    History

    Developed as a simpler and faster alternative to the full SOFA, which requires laboratory tests. qSOFA uses only 3 clinical criteria, assessable without the need for complementary exams.

    NANDA — Risk for shock

    Risk of inadequate blood flow to body tissues, which may lead to potentially fatal cellular dysfunction.

    NANDA — Ineffective breathing pattern

    Inspiration and/or expiration that does not provide adequate ventilation.

    NANDA — Risk for infection

    Increased susceptibility to invasion by pathogenic organisms.

    NOC — Risk control: sepsis

    • Vital signs monitored
    • Level of consciousness assessed
    • Infectious focus identified early

    NIC — Vital Signs Monitoring

    • Check mental status and level of consciousness
    • Monitor respiratory rate
    • Monitor blood pressure continuously
    • Immediately report changes to medical team

    NIC — Infection control

    • Collect cultures before antibiotic therapy
    • Administer antibiotics per prescription and protocol
    • Monitor signs of clinical worsening

    Scientific Validation

    Multicenter studies have shown that qSOFA ≥ 2 is associated with higher hospital mortality risk and prolonged ICU stay in patients with suspected infection outside the ICU.

    Sensitivity: 61% · Specificity: 72%

    Limitations

    qSOFA has moderate sensitivity and should not be used alone to rule out sepsis — a low score does not exclude the disease. It is a screening tool, not diagnostic, and should be complemented by clinical judgment and other institutional protocols (e.g., SIRS, serum lactate).

    How qSOFA works

    qSOFA (quick SOFA) evaluates 3 clinical criteria at the bedside: altered mental status, respiratory rate ≥22 breaths/min, and systolic blood pressure ≤100 mmHg. Each present criterion adds 1 point, totaling 0 to 3 points.

    Quick guide: 0-1 = Low risk | 2-3 = High risk, activate sepsis protocol.

    Does qSOFA diagnose sepsis?

    No. qSOFA is a rapid screening tool that identifies patients with suspected infection at higher risk of unfavorable outcome. The diagnosis of sepsis requires a complete clinical assessment and specific criteria (Sepsis-3).

    What is the difference between qSOFA and full SOFA?

    Full SOFA assesses organ dysfunction through 6 systems using laboratory tests, used in the ICU setting. qSOFA is a simplified version with only 3 clinical criteria, intended for rapid screening outside the ICU.
