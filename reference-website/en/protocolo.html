Neonatal Resuscitation Protocol

Ministry of Health guidelines for newborn resuscitation in the delivery room, based on the Apgar score and step-by-step clinical assessment.

Version: MS 2014 Updated: July 2026

Source

Ministry of Health

Application

Delivery room

Team

Multiprofessional

Target time

60 seconds (1st minute)

Step-by-step

1

Initial Assessment (First 30 seconds)

Assess 3 key questions immediately after birth:

  • Is the newborn full-term?
  • Is the newborn breathing or crying?
  • Is there good muscle tone?

If YES to all → routine care. If NO to any → initiate resuscitation steps.

2

Tactile Stimulation and Warming (30-60s)

  • Dry and warm the newborn with a warm sheet
  • Tactile stimulation on the soles of the feet and back
  • Position head slightly extended (sniffing position)
  • Suction airway only if obstructed (avoid routine suctioning)
3

Assess — 3 Vital Signs

  • Breathing: Is it regular? Is there respiratory effort?
  • Heart rate: ≥100 bpm? (auscultation or cord)
  • Color: Is there central cyanosis?
4

Positive Pressure Ventilation (PPV)

Indicada se: respiração ausente/gasping OU FC <100 bpm OU cianose central persistente.

  • Face mask + self-inflating bag (21% O₂ for term; 30% for preterm)
  • Rate: 40-60 inflations/min
  • Initial pressure: 20-25 cmH₂O
  • Avaliar em 30 segundos — se FC <60, passar para Step 5
5

Chest Compressions

Indicada se: FC <60 bpm após 30s de VPP efetiva.

  • Technique: two thumbs (encircling) on the lower third of the sternum
  • Depth: 1/3 of the anteroposterior diameter
  • Rhythm: 3:1 (90 compressions + 30 ventilations = 120/min)
  • O₂ at 100% during compressions
  • Reassess every 60 seconds
6

Epinephrine and Intubation

Indicada se: FC <60 bpm após 60s de compressões + VPP.

  • Epinephrine 1:10,000 — 0.1-0.3 mL/kg IV (cord) or 0.5-1 mL/kg intratracheal
  • Consider endotracheal intubation
  • Reassess HR every 60 seconds
  • Consider volume expansion (10 mL/kg) if hypovolemia suspected
7

Post-Resuscitation

  • Continuous monitoring in neonatal ICU
  • Active thermoregulation (incubator)
  • Serial capillary blood glucose
  • Document Apgar at 1st, 5th, 10th, and 20th minute
  • Communicate with team and record all interventions
