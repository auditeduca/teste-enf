Privacy and security

Privacy Policy and LGPD

Your privacy is our absolute priority. We are committed to transparency and the protection of your data.

1. Introduction

Elisabeth Marques respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal data of users of our therapeutic platform. This policy describes how we collect, use, store, and protect your information, in compliance with the Brazilian General Date Protection Law (LGPD — Law 13.709/2018).

2. Date we collect

We collect only the data necessary for the provision of therapeutic services:

  • Identification data: full name, CPF, date of birth, contact.
  • Health data: clinical history, mood records, responses to scales and psychometric tests.
  • Usage data: access logs, platform interactions, preferences.
  • Payment data: processed by a secure gateway; we do not store card data.

3. Purpose of processing

Your data is used exclusively for:

  • Provision of contracted psychotherapy and neuropsychoanalysis services;
  • Communication between patient and therapist through the platform;
  • Issuance of invoices and receipts;
  • Continuous improvement of user experience;
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory obligations of the CFP.

4. Legal basis

The processing of sensitive health data is carried out based on art. 11, item II, of the LGPD, for the purposes of life protection and health care, with the free, specific, and informed consent of the data subject.

5. Sharing

We do not sell your data. Sharing occurs only:

  • With the therapist responsible for your care;
  • With essential service providers (hosting, payment), under a confidentiality agreement;
  • When required by law or court order.

6. Security

We adopt robust technical and administrative measures:

  • AES-256 encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest;
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for professionals;
  • Audit logs and continuous monitoring;
  • Encrypted and redundant backups;
  • Restricted access by the principle of least privilege.

7. Your rights (LGPD)

You have the right to:

  • Confirm the existence of processing;
  • Access your data;
  • Correct incomplete or outdated data;
  • Request anonymization, blocking, or deletion;
  • Revoke consent at any time;
  • Request data portability;
  • Obtain information about entities with which your data has been shared.

8. Retention

We retain your data for the minimum period required by the CFP (5 years after the end of care) or as required by law. After this period, the data is anonymized or securely deleted.

9. DPO Contact

To exercise your rights or clarify doubts about this policy, contact our Date Protection Officer (DPO):

10. Updates

This policy may be updated periodically. The date of the last revision will always be indicated at the end of this page.
