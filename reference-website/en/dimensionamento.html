About this tool

Calculate the number of nursing professionals (ST) required for a unit, based on the total nursing hours (THE) demanded by the patient profile (SCP) and the Marinho Constant (KM), according to the methodology of COFEN Resolution No. 543/2017. Recommended for nursing managers in planning and staffing of inpatient units, considering patient classification by care complexity.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–5.99: Small Team (up to 6 professionals)
  • 6–20.99: Medium Team (6 to 21 professionals)
  • 21–50.99: Large Team (21 to 51 professionals)
  • 51–100000: Very Large Team (above 51 professionals)

Important tips

  • The formula has 3 steps: TNH (total care hours demanded), KM (Marinho constant, which adjusts for 7-day coverage, absences, and workload) and QP = TNH × KM.
  • The IST (Technical Safety Index) can never be less than 15%, according to COFEN Resolution No. 543/2017.
  • For minimum and intermediate care, it is recommended that at least 33% of the team (minimum of 6) be nurses, with the remainder being nursing technicians/aides.
  • In units of higher complexity (high dependency, semi-intensive, intensive), the proportion of nurses should be higher, according to current regulatory parameters.

    Foundation

    Staffing follows three steps: (1) calculation of Total Nursing Hours (TNH), summing the product of the number of patients in each PCS category by the respective care time (Minimum=4h, Intermediate=6h, High Dependency=10h, Semi-Intensive=10h, Intensive=18h); (2) calculation of the Marinho Constant (KM = DS × (1+IST)/CHS); (3) calculation of Staff (QP = TNH × KM).

    History

    The methodology was originally proposed by Maria José Trevizan Marinho and incorporated into the regulations of the Federal Nursing Council, being consolidated in COFEN Resolution No. 543/2017 (later revised by COFEN Resolution No. 743/2024).

    NANDA — Risk for infection (related to staff overload)

    Increased risk of adverse events and infections related to inadequate staffing in the face of care demand.

    NOC — Staff adequacy

    • Staff calculated according to COFEN 543/2017 methodology
    • Nurse/technician ratio respected according to care complexity
    • Periodic reassessment of staffing according to changes in patient profile (PCS)

    NIC — Nursing human resources management

    • Classify patients in the unit according to the Patient Classification System (PCS)
    • Calculate the Total Nursing Hours (TNH) demanded by the patient profile
    • Apply the Marinho Constant (KM) considering days of operation, IST, and team workload
    • Distribute the staff (QP) among nurses and technicians/aides according to predominant complexity
    • Periodically reassess staffing and at each relevant change in the care profile

    Scientific Validation

    Official methodology adopted nationally for nursing staff sizing in health services, referenced by audits of the Regional Nursing Councils (COREN).

    Limitations

    The result represents the total theoretical number of professionals (nurses + technicians/aides); the percentage distribution between these categories depends on the predominant complexity and must follow current regulatory parameters. The weekly workload (CHS) and IST may vary according to labor legislation, collective bargaining, and institutional policy.

    How the Staffing Calculator (COFEN 543/2017) Works

    Calculates the Total Nursing Hours (THE) from the number of patients in each SCP category, applies the Marinho Constant (KM), and obtains the Staffing Table (ST = THE × KM).

    Formula: THE = (PCM×4 + PCI×6 + PCAD×10 + PCSI×10 + PCIt×18) | KM = DS×(1+IST)/CHS | ST = THE × KM.

    Does the ST result already come divided between nurses and technicians?

    No. The ST represents the total number of nursing professionals. The percentage distribution between nurses and technicians/assistants must follow regulatory parameters according to the predominant care complexity of the unit.

    How often should staffing be recalculated?

    Periodic reassessment is recommended (usually semiannually or annually) and whenever there is a relevant change in the complexity profile of patients served by the unit.
