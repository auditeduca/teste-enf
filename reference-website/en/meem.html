About this tool

Screen for global cognitive impairment by assessing orientation, registration, attention/calculation, recall, and language. Indicated for screening cognitive impairment in older adults, patients with suspected dementia, or as part of a broad neurological/geriatric assessment.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–17: Severe Cognitive Impairment
  • 18–23: Mild to Moderate Cognitive Impairment
  • 24–30: Normal Cognition

Important tips

  • MMSE performance is strongly influenced by education — use, whenever possible, education-adjusted cutoff points according to Brazilian validation (Bertolucci et al., 1994).
  • The general cutoffs (≥24 normal, 18-23 mild to moderate, <18 severe) do not consider education and should be interpreted with caution.
  • The MMSE is a screening tool, not a definitive diagnosis of dementia.
  • Consider factors such as sensory deficits (visual/auditory), fatigue, and anxiety, which may influence test performance.

    Foundation

    The MMSE assesses 7 cognitive domains — temporal orientation (5 points), spatial orientation (5 points), registration (3 points), attention and calculation (5 points), recall (3 points), and language (9 points, distributed across naming, repetition, command, reading, writing, and copying) — totaling 30 points.

    History

    Developed by Marshal and Susan Folstein in 1975, it is one of the most widely used cognitive screening instruments worldwide, due to its quick application and extensive validation across different populations and languages.

    NANDA — Impaired memory

    Inability to remember or retrieve pieces of information or previously learned behavioral skills.

    NANDA — Chronic confusion

    Irreversible, long-standing, and/or progressive deterioration of intellect and personality, characterized by decreased ability to interpret environmental stimuli and thought processes.

    NANDA — Risk for falls

    Increased susceptibility to falls related to identified cognitive impairment that may affect judgment and safety.

    NOC — Cognition

    • MMSE score assessed and monitored over time
    • Referral for specialized evaluation when indicated
    • Environment adapted for patient safety according to degree of impairment

    NIC — Cognitive stimulation

    • Apply the MMSE for cognitive impairment screening
    • Refer for specialized neuropsychological evaluation based on results
    • Educate family about communication and safety strategies
    • Investigate potentially reversible causes of cognitive impairment (medications, metabolic disorders, depression)
    • Periodically reassess the patient's cognitive progression

    Scientific Validation

    Extensively validated and translated into several languages, including Brazilian Portuguese, with education-adjusted cutoff points widely studied in the Brazilian population (Bertolucci et al., 1994; Brucki et al., 2003).

    Limitations

    MMSE performance is strongly influenced by education — the cutoff points presented here do not consider this adjustment. It is recommended, whenever possible, to use education-adjusted cutoff points according to Brazilian validation. It does not assess executive functions in depth.

    How the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) Works

    The MMSE scores points from 7 cognitive domains — temporal orientation (5), spatial orientation (5), registration (3), attention/calculation (5), recall (3), and language (9) — totaling 30 points.

    Quick guide (without education adjustment): ≥24 = Normal | 18-23 = Mild to moderate | <18 = Severe.

    Are the MMSE cutoff points the same for all education levels?

    They should not be. Education strongly influences test performance. Brazilian studies (Bertolucci et al., 1994; Brucki et al., 2003) propose education-adjusted cutoff points, which should be prioritized whenever available.

    Does the MMSE diagnose dementia?

    No. It is a cognitive screening tool that identifies patients requiring further evaluation. Dementia diagnosis requires a complete clinical assessment, complementary tests, and often specialized neuropsychological evaluation.

    Important Notice

    This tool is for educational and clinical decision support purposes. Results should always be reviewed and validated by a qualified nursing professional, according to patient assessment and institutional protocols.
