About this tool

Assess the patient's degree of independence in 8 instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs), which are more complex than the basic activities assessed by the Katz Index. Indicated for elderly patients or those with suspected functional impairment, as a complement to the assessment of basic activities of daily living (Katz/Barthel).

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–2: Severe Dependence
  • 3–5: Moderate Dependence
  • 6–8: Independence

Important tips

  • Always combine with the Katz Index for a complete functional assessment (basic ADLs + IADLs).
  • Assess actual observed or reported performance, not the patient's theoretical ability.
  • Dependence in IADLs usually precedes dependence in basic ADLs in the functional decline process.
  • Reassess periodically, especially after clinical events.

    Foundation

    The Lawton Scale was developed by M. Powell Lawton and Elaine Brody in 1969, assessing an individual's ability to perform instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs), which are more complex than basic self-care activities evaluated by the Katz Index.

    History

    Widely used in geriatrics and gerontology as a complement to Katz, providing a more complete assessment of the elderly's functionality, especially relevant for decisions about need for home support or institutionalization.

    NANDA — Impaired Home Maintenance

    Inability to independently maintain a safe and growth-promoting immediate domestic environment.

    NANDA — Readiness for Enhanced Self-Health Management

    A pattern of regulating and integrating into daily living a therapeutic regimen for treatment of illness that can be strengthened.

    NOC — Self-Care: Instrumental Activities of Daily Living

    • Manages medications correctly
    • Performs shopping and meal preparation
    • Manages personal finances
    • Uses transportation independently

    NIC — Self-Care Assistance: IADLs

    • Assess functional capacity for instrumental activities
    • Identify need for home support
    • Educate on correct and safe medication use
    • Involve family/caregiver in planning necessary support

    Scientific Validation

    Validated in various populations, with good interobserver reliability, widely used in comprehensive geriatric assessments.

    Limitations

    May be influenced by cultural and gender factors in the original version (some tasks historically assigned differently between men and women); the simplified and dichotomized version used in this calculator is the most commonly adopted in current clinical practice, applied equally to both sexes.

    How the Lawton Scale works

    The Lawton Scale assesses 8 instrumental activities of daily living — telephone use, shopping, meal preparation, housekeeping, laundry, transportation, responsibility for medications, and handling finances — classifying each as independent (1 point) or dependent (0 points). The sum ranges from 0 to 8 points.

    Quick guide: 6-8 = Independence | 3-5 = Moderate dependence | 0-2 = Severe dependence.

    Is dependence in IADLs more severe than in ADLs?

    Not necessarily more severe, but it usually appears earlier in the functional decline process. Loss of independence in IADLs (complex activities) often precedes loss of independence in basic ADLs (self-care).

    Can the Lawton Scale be administered by family members?

    Yes, especially in patients with cognitive impairment, information can be obtained from a caregiver or family member who knows the patient's routine well, complementing self-assessment when possible.
