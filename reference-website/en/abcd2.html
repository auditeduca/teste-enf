About this tool

Estimate the risk of stroke in the days following a transient ischemic attack (TIA), guiding the need for hospitalization and urgent investigation. Indicated for patients with a recent diagnosis of transient ischemic attack in the emergency department or neurology outpatient clinic.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–3: Low Risk
  • 4–5: Moderate Risk
  • 6–7: High Risk

Important tips

  • Even low ABCD2 score patients may require urgent investigation if high-risk findings are present on neuroimaging or cardiac evaluation.
  • Secondary prevention (antiplatelets, statins, blood pressure control) should be initiated as early as possible after TIA.
  • Reassess the patient clinically if new neurological symptoms arise, regardless of the initial score.
  • The ABCD2 is an initial screening tool, not a substitute for complete etiological investigation of TIA.

    Foundation

    The ABCD2 Score was developed by Rothwell and colleagues, combining 5 factors — Age, Blood pressure, Clinical features, Duration of symptoms, and Diabetes — to estimate the risk of stroke in the days following a transient ischemic attack.

    History

    Widely used in emergency and neurology services for urgent triage of investigation and determination of need for hospitalization after a TIA, contributing to early prevention of subsequent stroke.

    NANDA — Risk for Ineffective Cerebral Tissue Perfusion

    At risk for decrease in cerebral blood circulation that may compromise health.

    NANDA — Anxiety

    Vague, uneasy feeling of discomfort or dread related to the risk of a new neurological event.

    NANDA — Deficient Knowledge

    Absence or deficiency of cognitive information related to risk factors and warning signs of stroke.

    NOC — Tissue Perfusion: Cerebral

    • Absence of new neurological deficits
    • Controlled blood pressure
    • Adherence to investigation and preventive treatment

    NIC — Neurologic Monitoring

    • Apply the ABCD2 Score in the initial assessment of TIA
    • Monitor vital signs and neurological status serially
    • Assist in urgent diagnostic investigation (imaging, carotid evaluation, cardiac)
    • Educate the patient about warning signs of stroke and need for immediate return

    Scientific Validation

    Validated in multiple international studies, demonstrating good risk discrimination ability, especially for outcomes at 2 and 7 days after TIA.

    Limitations

    Some studies suggest that even low-score patients may benefit from early investigation and preventive treatment, especially when there are high-risk signs on neuroimaging (significant stenosis, cardioembolic event). The score should not be the sole decision criterion.

    How the ABCD2 Score Works

    The ABCD2 Score sums points from 5 factors — age, blood pressure, clinical features, symptom duration, and diabetes — totaling 0 to 7 points, which estimate the risk of stroke in the days following a transient ischemic attack.

    Quick guide: 0-3 = Low risk | 4-5 = Moderate risk | 6-7 = High risk.

    Does the ABCD2 Score replace the etiological investigation of TIA?

    No. It is an initial risk screening tool. Complete investigation (neuroimaging, carotid and cardiac evaluation) is essential to identify the cause of TIA and guide secondary prevention, regardless of the score.

    Does a low ABCD2 score mean there is no risk of stroke?

    It does not eliminate risk, only indicates a relatively lower risk in the first few days. Some low-score patients may still have high-risk findings on additional tests, justifying early investigation and preventive treatment.
