Updated NR-1 - New Horizons in Worker Mental Health

Updated NR-1

"New Horizons" in Worker Mental Health. Understand the changes to Regulatory Standard No. 1.

NR-1 and the Revolution in Mental Health Protection in the Workplace

The updated NR-1 has officially included psychosocial risks in Occupational Risk Management (GRO). The standard now requires companies to adopt measures to prevent problems such as stress, moral harassment, excessive pressure, and work overload. The change strengthens mental health protection at work and expands the role of Occupational Health Nursing in preventing occupational diseases and promoting Occupational Safety and Health (OSH).

1. WHAT IS NR-1 AND WHY HAS IT CHANGED

Publication: MTb Ordinance No. 3.214/1978 | Update: 2024/2025

NR-1 — General Provisions and Occupational Risk Management — is the mother standard of all NRs. It establishes the risk management system that companies must adopt to ensure worker safety and health. The most recent update brought clear requirements regarding:

  • Assessment and control of psychosocial risks (stress, harassment, burnout)
  • Psychological support and counseling programs for workers
  • Management of work hours and adequate rest
  • Effective worker participation in OSH decisions
  • Integration of physical and mental health in the work environment

2. WORKER MENTAL HEALTH: THE NEW FOCUS OF NR-1

The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that depression and anxiety cost the global economy about US$ 1 trillion per year in lost productivity. In Brazil, absenteeism due to mental disorders increased by 28% between 2019 and 2024. The updated NR-1 responds to this reality by requiring companies to:

Main Psychosocial Risks addressed:

  • Occupational stress: high demand x low control
  • Burnout: emotional exhaustion, depersonalization, low accomplishment
  • Moral and sexual harassment: violence in the workplace
  • Work overload: excessive hours and unrealistic goals
  • Job insecurity: precariousness and future uncertainties

3. Change: Integration between NR-1 and NR-17

NR-1 now works in an integrated manner with NR-17 (Ergonomics); NR-17 organizes working conditions into five areas: Work organization, Lifting and transporting materials, Furniture, Machinery and tools, Comfort in the work environment.

Psychosocial factors are mainly linked to work organization.

  • Abusive goals
  • Excessive demands
  • Lack of role clarity
  • Poor management;
  • Little autonomy
  • Internal conflicts
  • Isolated work
  • Poor communication
  • These factors can cause: anxiety, stress, depression, etc.

4. Continuous improvement process (PDCA)

NR-1 determines that the company maintain continuous improvement in occupational health and safety using the PDCA model:

  • Plan: Identify psychosocial risks and define preventive measures
  • Do: Implement actions in the company.
  • Check: Verify if the measures actually worked.
  • Act: Correct failures and continuously improve the process.

Important: Reception does not replace psychiatric or psychotherapeutic treatment, but serves as a bridge for continuous care. The nurse must record the reception in the occupational medical record and monitor progress.

4. PSYCHOLOGICAL SUPPORT: MANDATORY AND PRACTICE

The updated NR-1 makes psychological reception mandatory for workers in situations of emotional vulnerability. This includes:

  • Return to work after leave due to mental disorder
  • Workers victims of moral or sexual harassment
  • Professionals exposed to traumatic events at work
  • Situations of organizational restructuring (layoffs, mergers)
  • Workers with recognized burnout

Important: Reception does not replace psychiatric or psychotherapeutic treatment, but serves as a bridge for continuous care. The nurse must record the reception in the occupational medical record and monitor progress.

5. Risk probability assessment

NR-1 also now requires that psychosocial risks be assessed within the ergonomic analysis and instruments and methods used to evaluate the worker's mental health, such as the use of surveys to assess the worker's mental health status:

Job Content Questionnaire (JCQ):

Assesses psychological demand, control, and social support at work.

Copenhagen Psychosocial Questionnaire (COPSOQ):

Comprehensive assessment of psychosocial factors at work.

Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI):

Gold standard instrument for burnout assessment.

Patient Health Questionnaire (PHQ-9):

Screening for depressive symptoms.

6. RISK MANAGEMENT PROGRAM (PGR) AND MENTAL HEALTH

The PGR is the central document of NR-1. With the update, it must explicitly include:

  • Psychosocial risk map by sector and function
  • Action plan to mitigate identified risks
  • Mental health indicators (absenteeism, turnover, leaves)
  • Periodic reassessment schedule
  • Responsible parties for intervention actions

Nurse's Checklist in the PGR:

  • Are psychosocial risks mapped by category?
  • Is there an action plan with defined deadlines?
  • Are there quantitative mental health indicators?
  • Does the PCMSO include screening for mental disorders?
  • Is there a channel for reporting workplace harassment?
  • Have managers been trained in reception?

7. LEGAL FRAMEWORK AND NORMATIVE REFERENCES

NR-1 does not act in isolation. Its application integrates with other standards and legislation:

Complementary Standards:

  • NR-7 — Occupational Medical Control Program
  • NR-9 — Environmental Risk Prevention Program
  • NR-17 — Ergonomics (relationship with work organization)
  • NR-35 — Work at Height (stress and anxiety)

Applicable Legislation:

  • Law No. 6,514/1977 (SST standards)
  • Law No. 13,467/2017 (Labor Reform — harassment)
  • Law No. 14,457/2022 (telework and working hours)
  • Federal Constitution — Articles 6 and 7 (human dignity)

8. Important: do not confuse with psychological examination

Psychosocial risk assessment is not a mental health medical exam; it is not intended to diagnose workers. The goal is to prevent illness by improving working conditions. Medical exams remain the responsibility of the PCMSO and NR-7.

The objective is not to assess the worker's mental health... The focus is to improve working conditions and prevent illness.
