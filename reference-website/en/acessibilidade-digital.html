Commitment to Digital Accessibility

We believe that nursing information and knowledge should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities or limitations. Therefore, we continuously invest in digital accessibility.

What is digital accessibility?

Digital accessibility consists of removing barriers that may prevent people with disabilities from interacting with online content and tools. This includes visual, auditory, motor, cognitive disabilities, and other temporary or permanent conditions.

Our commitment

We are committed to meeting the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA, and to continuously evolving our platform to ensure the best experience for all users.

  • Complete and intuitive keyboard navigation;
  • Compatibility with screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver);
  • Adequate contrast and high contrast option;
  • Descriptive texts in images and non-text elements;
  • Clear semantic and hierarchical structure.

Continuous improvements

We conduct periodic tests with real users and automated tools to identify opportunities for improvement. If you encounter any barriers, please contact us.

Available Accessibility Resources

Learn about the resources already implemented on our platform to make your navigation more inclusive and efficient.

Keyboard navigation

All interactive elements (links, buttons, form fields) can be accessed and activated using only the keyboard, using the Tab key to navigate and Enter ou Space to activate.

High contrast and dark mode

In the accessibility bar (gear icon), you can activate high contrast e o dark mode, which change background and text colors for better readability.

Font and typography adjustment

In the same preferences modal, you can increase or decrease font size up to 200% and activate the dyslexia-friendly font, which uses an easy-to-read typography.

Screen reader compatibility

The HTML code structure is semantic and includes ARIA attributes to ensure that screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver) correctly interpret elements, headings, regions, and states.

Alternative texts and captions

All informative images have descriptive alternative text. Videos and multimedia content are captioned or accompanied by text transcription.

Keyboard Guides and Shortcuts

Here you will find a list of the main keyboard shortcuts and tips to optimize your navigation on the platform.

General shortcuts

  • Tab — Navigate between links and controls;
  • Shift + Tab — Navigate backwards;
  • Enter ou Space — Activate button or link;
  • Esc — Close menus, modals, and the mobile menu;
  • Arrow keys ← → — Move between menu items (when expanded);
  • Ctrl + + / Ctrl + - — Increase/decrease browser zoom.

Accessibility bar shortcuts

  • Alt + A — Open accessibility preferences modal;
  • Alt + L — Activate/deactivate reading mode (focus on content);
  • Alt + P — Print the current page.

These shortcuts are subject to operating system and browser settings.

Tips for more accessible navigation

  • Use the dark mode to reduce eye strain;
  • Activate the high contrast if you need more distinction between colors;
  • Increase the font size for more comfortable reading;
  • If using a screen reader, make sure it is configured with the voice and speed that best meet your needs.

Accessibility Support Tools

In addition to native resources, we provide tools and guidance so you can further customize your experience.

Accessibility preferences modal

Click the gear icon () in the utility bar to access a panel where you can adjust font size, enable high contrast, dark mode, and dyslexia-friendly font. Settings are saved locally in your browser.

Read aloud

The Read button in the utility bar uses the browser's speech synthesis API to read the main page content aloud. You can pause, resume, or stop reading at any time.

Sharing and saving

Use the Share, Save e Print buttons to export content in an accessible format, whether for offline reading or use on other devices.
