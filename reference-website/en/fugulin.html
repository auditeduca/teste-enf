About this tool

Classify the patient according to their degree of dependence on nursing care, supporting the sizing of nursing staff in care units. Indicated for daily use in inpatient units as a management tool for nursing staff sizing, as recommended by COFEN.

Interpretation ranges

  • 9–14: Minimal Care (PCM)
  • 15–20: Intermediate Care (PCI)
  • 21–26: High Dependency Care (PCAD)
  • 27–31: Semi-Intensive Care (PCSI)
  • 32–36: Intensive Care (PCIt)

Important tips

  • Reassess classification daily, as the patient's clinical condition may change rapidly.
  • Use classification to support, not replace, the nurse's judgment regarding resource needs.
  • Document classification consistently to allow time-series analysis of the unit.
  • Consult COFEN Resolution 543/2017 for the sizing parameters associated with each category.

    Foundation

    The Fugulin Patient Classification System, developed by Fugulin and collaborators based on studies by Perroca, evaluates 9 care areas (mental status, oxygenation, vital signs, motility, ambulation, feeding, body care, elimination, and therapeutics), each scored from 1 to 4, to classify the patient's degree of dependence on nursing care.

    History

    Widely used in Brazil as a management tool to support nursing staff sizing, referenced by COFEN Resolution No. 543/2017, which addresses parameters for nursing staff sizing.

    NANDA — Risk for disuse syndrome

    Risk of deterioration of body systems due to prescribed or unavoidable musculoskeletal inactivity, applicable according to the identified degree of dependence.

    NOC — Identified Level of Care Needed

    • Daily updated dependency classification
    • Nursing resources compatible with patient needs

    NIC — Nursing Resource Management

    • Classify the patient daily according to the degree of dependence
    • Subsidize nursing staff sizing for the shift/unit
    • Communicate relevant changes in classification to the management team
    • Document the classification in the medical record/institutional system

    Scientific Validation

    Validated and widely used in Brazilian healthcare institutions as a standardized patient classification tool, supporting nursing human resource management decisions.

    Limitations

    Classification should be reassessed daily, as the patient's dependency level may change rapidly according to clinical evolution. Staff sizing parameters associated with each category vary according to current legislation and institutional norms.

    How the Fugulin Classification System works

    The Fugulin System sums points from 9 care areas — mental status, oxygenation, vital signs, motility, ambulation, feeding, body care, elimination, and therapeutics — each scored from 1 to 4, totaling 9 to 36 points, which classify the patient into 5 nursing care dependency categories.

    Quick guide: 9-14 = Minimal | 15-20 = Intermediate | 21-26 = High Dependency | 27-31 = Semi-Intensive | 32-36 = Intensive.

    How often should the Fugulin classification be reapplied?

    Daily reassessment is recommended, usually once per shift or per day, according to institutional protocol, as the patient's dependency level may change rapidly.

    Does the Fugulin System replace the nurse's clinical judgment?

    No. It is an objective management support tool, but the responsible nurse should always consider the patient's complete clinical context when making decisions about care and resource allocation.
