About this tool

Estimate 10-year mortality risk from the weighted sum of present comorbidities and patient age. Indicated for risk stratification in patients with multiple comorbidities, clinical research, risk adjustment in epidemiological studies, and care planning.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–0: No Relevant Comorbidity
  • 1–2: Mild Comorbidity
  • 3–4: Moderate Comorbidity
  • 5–37: Severe Comorbidity

Important tips

  • Consider only documented and confirmed comorbidities, not suspected ones.
  • Age adjustment is cumulative with comorbidities — add both for the final score.
  • Use the index to support prognostic discussion, never in isolation for therapeutic decisions.
  • Reassess the index if new comorbidities are diagnosed during follow-up.

    Foundation

    The Charlson Comorbidity Index was developed by Mary Charlson and colleagues in 1987, assigning weights from 1 to 6 points to 19 comorbidity categories, with additional adjustment per decade of age starting at 50 years, to estimate 10-year mortality risk.

    History

    One of the most widely used comorbidity indices globally in clinical and epidemiological research, employed for risk adjustment in observational studies and for prognostic stratification in clinical practice.

    Scientific Validation

    Extensively validated across diverse populations and clinical settings, with good correlation between the score and observed mortality, although performance may vary depending on the population and outcome studied.

    Limitations

    Does not encompass all possible comorbidities or their detailed individual severity. Originally developed to predict 10-year mortality, it may have lower accuracy for short-term outcomes or populations very different from the original cohort.

    How the Charlson Index Works

    The Charlson Index sums points (1, 2, 3, or 6, depending on severity) assigned to specific comorbidities present in the patient, added to additional points per decade of age from 50 years, generating a total score associated with 10-year mortality risk.

    Quick guide: 0 = No relevant comorbidity | 1-2 = Mild | 3-4 = Moderate | ≥5 = Severe.

    Does the Charlson Index predict mortality at any time frame?

    The index was originally developed and validated to estimate 10-year mortality. Its use for short-term outcomes should be interpreted with caution.

    Is age adjustment mandatory?

    The most widely used version in practice and research is the age-adjusted version, which adds additional points per decade of life from age 50, in addition to the comorbidity score.
