About this tool

Assess the patient's fall risk, especially older adults, based on 5 domains: previous falls, use of risk medications, sensory deficit, mental state, and ambulation pattern. Indicated on hospital admission, especially for elderly patients, and periodically according to institutional fall prevention protocol.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–2: Low Risk
  • 3–7: High Risk

Important tips

  • A score of 3 or more is sufficient to classify the patient as high risk.
  • Review the list of medications in use, as several common drugs in older adults increase fall risk.
  • Combine the assessment with physical examination of gait and balance.
  • Reassess after any change in the patient's clinical status or medication regimen.

    Foundation

    The Downton Index was developed to assess fall risk in the elderly, considering 5 domains: history of previous falls, use of risk medications, sensory deficit, mental state, and gait pattern, with a total score ranging from 0 to 7 (or 11, depending on the version used).

    History

    Widely used in geriatric services and patient safety protocols for fall risk screening, especially in hospitalized elderly populations.

    NANDA — Risk for falls

    Increased vulnerability to falls, which can cause physical harm and compromise health.

    NANDA — Impaired physical mobility

    Limitation in independent voluntary movement of body parts or the whole body.

    NOC — Fall prevention behavior

    • Correct use of assistive devices
    • Requests assistance when needed
    • Risk-free environment identified

    NIC — Fall prevention

    • Identify cognitive, sensory, and motor deficits
    • Review medications associated with fall risk with medical team
    • Flag fall risk at bedside and in medical record
    • Educate patient and family about safety measures
    • Keep bed rails raised and bed in low position when indicated

    Scientific Validation

    Validation studies report good sensitivity for identifying patients at risk of falling, with a cutoff of 3 points or more indicating high risk.

    Limitations

    Like most fall risk scales, it has limited specificity and may classify a significant number of patients as high risk. It should be complemented by individualized clinical assessment and evaluation of the physical environment.

    How the Downton Index works

    The Downton Index sums points from 5 domains — previous falls, use of risk medications, sensory deficit, mental state, and ambulation pattern — totaling 0 to 7 points.

    Quick guide: 0-2 = Low risk | ≥3 = High risk, implement intensive fall prevention protocol.

    Does the Downton Index replace individual clinical assessment?

    No. It is a screening tool that should be combined with clinical judgment, assessment of the physical environment, and detailed patient history.

    How often should I reassess the Downton Index?

    Reassessment is recommended on admission, after any fall episode, change in medication regimen, and periodically according to institutional protocol.
