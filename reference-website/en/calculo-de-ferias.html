About this tool

Estimate the total vacation amount, including the constitutional additional 1/3, based on the base salary and the number of vacation days the worker is entitled to. Useful for nursing professionals and other workers to verify the amount received for vacation, whether taken or sold (pecuniary leave).

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–999999: Estimated Vacation Amount + 1/3

Important tips

  • Vacation payment must be made up to 2 days before the start of the vacation period.
  • The total vacation amount is the same whether the period is fully taken or partially converted into pecuniary allowance.
  • If there are averages of overtime, night shift differential, or habitual commissions, they must be included in the actual calculation base, in addition to the base salary.
  • In case of proportional vacation (less than 12 months worked), adjust the number of vacation days entered.

    Foundation

    The right to annual paid vacation with at least 1/3 more than the normal salary is provided for in Art. 7, item XVII, of the Federal Constitution of 1988, and regulated by Arts. 129 to 145 of the CLT. After each 12-month work period (acquisition period), the worker is entitled to 30 days of vacation, and may convert up to 1/3 of this period into pecuniary allowance (sale of vacation).

    History

    The pecuniary allowance (sale of up to 10 vacation days) does not represent additional payment beyond the normal vacation amount — it merely converts rest days into advance payment, maintaining the total amount proportional to the 30-day entitlement, always plus the constitutional 1/3 on the total.

    Scientific Validation

    The calculation may be proportional in cases of incomplete vacation (less than 12 months worked) or contract termination, requiring adjustment according to the number of months/days worked in the acquisition period.

    Limitations

    This calculator provides a simplified estimate for educational and verification purposes, considering only the reported base salary. It does not replace the official payroll calculation or guidance from an accounting or labor law professional. Averages of habitual additional payments (overtime, night shift differential) must be included in the actual vacation calculation base.

    How Vacation Calculation Works

    The vacation amount is calculated from the daily salary (base salary ÷ 30), multiplied by the number of vacation days the worker is entitled to, plus the constitutional additional 1/3 of that amount.

    Formula: Vacation Amount = (Salary ÷ 30) × Vacation Days × 4/3 (where 4/3 represents the full amount plus the additional 1/3).

    Does this calculator replace the official payroll calculation?

    No. It is an educational tool for estimation and verification. The official calculation must consider averages of habitual additional payments and other contractual particularities, being the responsibility of the company's HR department.

    Is it possible to sell all vacation days?

    No. The CLT allows the conversion into pecuniary leave of at most 1/3 of the vacation period (i.e., up to 10 days out of a total of 30), with at least 20 days of effective rest being mandatory.
