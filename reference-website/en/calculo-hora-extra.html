About this tool

Estimate the value of overtime hours worked, based on base salary, number of overtime hours, and applicable overtime percentage. Useful for nursing professionals and other workers who perform overtime, to verify the amount received on pay stub.

Important tips

  • Confirm the salary divisor applicable to your category (220h is the standard for a 44-hour workweek).
  • Check the category's collective agreement, which may provide a percentage higher than the constitutional minimum of 50%.
  • If the company adopts a hours bank, overtime may be compensated with time off instead of payment — check the current agreement.
  • Regular overtime is included in the calculation of vacation pay, 13th salary, and FGTS.

    Foundation

    The right to overtime pay with a minimum 50% increase over the normal hourly rate is provided in Art. 7, item XVI, of the Federal Constitution of 1988 and regulated by Art. 59 of the CLT. The divisor 220 corresponds to the standard monthly workload of 44 hours per week (220 = 44 hours × 5 weeks, approximately).

    History

    Collective bargaining agreements often establish a 100% percentage for overtime performed on Sundays and holidays, although this percentage is not an automatic federal legal minimum, depending on the professional category.

    Scientific Validation

    The calculation may vary according to the salary divisor applicable to the category (220h is the most common for a 44-hour workweek, but other divisors may apply for different workloads, such as 200h or 180h).

    Limitations

    This calculator provides a simplified estimate for educational and verification purposes. It does not replace the official payroll calculation or guidance from an accounting or labor law professional. When applicable, the hours bank may change the way overtime is compensated.

    How Overtime Calculation Works

    The overtime value is calculated from the normal hourly rate (salary ÷ 220, for a 44-hour workweek), multiplied by the number of overtime hours worked, and increased by the applicable overtime percentage (minimum 50%, may be higher per collective agreement or work on Sundays/holidays).

    Formula: Overtime Value = (Salary ÷ 220) × Overtime Hours × (1 + Percentage/100).

    Does this calculator replace the official payroll calculation?

    No. It is an educational tool for estimation and verification. The official calculation must be performed by the company's HR department, considering the collective bargaining agreement and applicable salary divisor.

    Do habitual overtime hours affect the value of other labor entitlements?

    Yes. Habitual overtime hours are included in the calculation basis for vacation pay, 13th salary, FGTS, and may impact severance payments, according to consolidated understanding in Labor Courts.
