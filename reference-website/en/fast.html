About this tool

Rapidly recognize suspected stroke using 3 clinical findings (face, arm, speech), emphasizing the importance of time for treatment initiation. Indicated for rapid stroke suspicion recognition by lay public, first responders, and prehospital emergency teams.

Interpretation ranges

  • 0–0: No Warning Signs
  • 1–3: Suspected Stroke - Time is Critical

Important tips

  • FAST is a mnemonic: Face, Arm, Speech, and Time — the first 3 are tested, the T reinforces urgency.
  • The presence of any of the 3 altered findings is sufficient to strongly suspect stroke and activate emergency services.
  • Always record the exact time of symptom onset — this information is essential for decisions about reperfusion therapies (thrombolysis/thrombectomy).
  • FAST may have lower sensitivity for posterior circulation strokes — variations such as BE-FAST (which adds Balance and Eyes) aim to increase detection of these cases.

    Foundation

    O FAST avalia 3 achados clínicos — Face (rosto), Arm (braço) e Speech (fala) — cada um pontuado como 0 (normal) ou 1 (alterado), somando de 0 a 3 abnormal findings. O 'T' de Time (tempo) não é pontuado, mas reforça a urgência de buscar atendimento médico imediato diante de qualquer achado alterado.

    History

    O mnemônico FAST foi popularizado no Reino Unido como campanha de conscientização pública para reconhecimento rápido de AVC, derivado da Escala Pré-Hospitalar de Cincinnati (CPSS), com o acréscimo do 'T' de Time para reforçar a urgência do tratamento.

    NANDA — Ineffective cerebral tissue perfusion

    Reduced cerebral blood circulation, capable of compromising health, suspected by findings of facial, arm, and/or speech abnormalities.

    NOC — Tissue Perfusion: Cerebral

    • FAST applied rapidly upon any suspicion of acute neurological deficit
    • Time of symptom onset accurately recorded
    • Emergency services activated immediately when indicated

    NIC — Neurologic emergency management

    • Apply FAST rapidly upon any suspicion of stroke
    • Accurately record the time of symptom onset or last known well time
    • Immediately activate emergency services when any finding is abnormal
    • Keep the person fasting, without administering food, liquids, or medications orally
    • Educate family/community on early recognition of stroke signs

    Scientific Validation

    Widely used in public awareness campaigns and by emergency teams, with good sensitivity for detecting anterior circulation stroke. Has high sensitivity, being an effective tool for rapid initial triage.

    Limitations

    May have lower sensitivity for posterior circulation/cerebellar strokes, which may present with different symptoms (dizziness, incoordination, visual changes) not captured by the 3 classic findings. Some variations include additional items (e.g., BE-FAST, which adds Balance and Eyes) to increase sensitivity.

    How the FAST Scale Works

    FAST assesses 3 findings — Face, Arm, and Speech — each scored as 0 (normal) or 1 (abnormal), reinforcing the importance of Time to seek immediate care.

    Quick guide: 0 = No warning signs | 1-3 = Suspected stroke, time is critical — activate emergency services immediately.

    What is the difference between FAST and the Cincinnati Prehospital Stroke Scale (CPSS)?

    O FAST e a CPSS avaliam essencialmente os mesmos 3 achados clínicos (face, braço, fala), sendo o FAST popularizado como ferramenta de conscientização pública com o acréscimo do 'T' de Time para reforçar a urgência do atendimento.

    Are there variations of FAST with more items?

    Yes. Variations such as BE-FAST add assessment of Balance and Eyes, aiming to increase sensitivity for detecting posterior circulation strokes, which may not be captured by the 3 classic FAST findings.
